Urban Farm

The HBHS Green Team Urban Farm was founded in 2014

Urban Farm Retrofit

In 2014, we built state-of-the-art aquaponic and hydroponic systems that maximize efficiency in an urban environment. In a combined effort between local donations and student work, the systems were up and running in months.

Aquaponics

Aquaponics is a system where fish and plants coexist symbiotically. Excrement from the fish is pumped up to raised vegetable beds to be used as nutrients. In return, the beds clean the water that is then sent back to the fish tank. This form of urban agriculture is an ideal closed loop sustainable system that drastically reduces water consumption while increasing production.

Hydroponics

Hydroponics utilizes a series of vertical towers that are irrigated by nutrient-laden water. This system is ideal for growing produce in urban environments to maximize efficiency in limited space.
